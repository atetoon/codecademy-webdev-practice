An Insider’s Guide to Surviving Engineering

Engineering can be both insanely exciting & brutally overwhelming, especially in your first year. Luckily, I’m here to break things down — from coding fundamentals to real skill-building — so you don’t just pass exams, but actually become dangerous with your skills.

Mastering the Core Skills

First rule: stop chasing everything. Focus on fundamentals — C++, DSA, problem-solving, and understanding how things actually work. Don’t just watch tutorials. Build. Break things. Fix them. That’s how real learning happens.

Hackathons & Real Projects

Hackathons and personal projects separate average students from builders. You don’t need 10 certificates — you need 2–3 solid projects. Deploy something. Host it. Make it real. That’s what makes recruiters notice.
